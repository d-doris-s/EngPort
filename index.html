ABOUT ME

My name is Doris Siu, and I'm a junior ('27) studying Mechanical Engineering at Boston University with a concentration in Manufacturing. For as long as I can remember, I've always loved DIY projects and designing new inventions, sometimes to make the day-to-day life a little easier, and sometimes to make things look and feel better.

When I was 12, I built phone stands out of cardboard and cord holders out of paper. Simple, and admittedly kind of ugly, but they worked. Over the years, I kept revisiting the phone stand and improving the design with new materials and styles. With each version, it became stronger, more adaptable, and prettier. I share this story because I believe it captures who I am as an engineer: someone who is always iterating, learning, and refining.

Now, with access to machinery and more resilient materials, I've taken that childhood project further. One of my favorite builds has been a motion-tracking phone mount that automatically follows a subject's movements. Instead of cardboard and paper, I used SolidWorks, 3D printing, laser cutting, and electronics to create a durable and intricate design.

Looking ahead, I want to grow as an engineer who designs products and improves everyday life, taking ideas from concept to reality and refining them along the way with technical expertise, persistence, and creativity.
