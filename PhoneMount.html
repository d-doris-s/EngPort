Motion-Tracking Phone Holder

May 2025 - August 2025

This device automatically rotates to follow a moving subject. A phone can be mounted on top, while a webcam captures real-time subject position data. The data is processed through a computer vision algorithm and sent to an Arduino Uno, which controls a servo motor to adjust the rotation accordingly. I designed the housing in SolidWorks and fabricated it with a combination of 3D-printed and laser-cut components.

Inspired by ballroom dance culture, where dancers often record their performances, this prototype was developed under a self-imposed $100 budget. While the system currently tracks subjects within 6 ft with limited accuracy, it demonstrates a functional proof of concept.
